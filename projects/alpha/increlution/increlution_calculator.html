Increlution Calculator

An introduction to Increlution

Increlution is an idle game based on survival & time management.

There are many objectives in Increlution that can be made up. One of them is lifetime.

See, Increlution is a game where your health bar is on a timer. More specifically, you have a consistent health decay (HD).

At the beginning of Increlution, your HD starts at 0.50 HP/s (health per second). This value is smooth, so it will be processed every frame (1/60th of a second).

Additionally, this value increases by 25% per minute. This increase is compounding, so it increases by more over time. This increase is also smooth, so it will also be processed each frame.

During your first game of Increlution, your max health is 100. This means that with a HD of 0.50 HP/s, you would survive for 200 seconds, right? That would only be true if HD was a constant value of 0.50 HP/s.

I wanted to know how long I would truly survive on these conditions, so I decided to try & calculate an estimated lifetime.

Calculating estimated lifetime : Part 1 - Idling

Now that you understand the values, its time to attempt to calculate an estimated lifetime. This value will assume no changes to HD other than its natural increase & health (HP) is initially equal to 100.

My approach to this was to iterate frame-by-frame to keep getting new HD values until HP is less than or equal to 0. Here's how that went :

Firstly, we need to understand how much HD is naturally increasing every frame.

(+25%/minute)/(3600 frames/minute) = +0.00694%/frame

So now we know that HD increases by 0.00694% every frame, which can also be written as *1.0000694/frame.

Frame 1 : [HP - HD] 100 HP - (0.50 HD/second)/(60 frames per second) = 0.0083 HD/frame = 99.9917 HP; [HD Natural Increase] 0.50 HD/s *1.0000694/f = 0.5000347 HD/s

Frame 2 : [HP - HD] 99.9917 HP - (0.5000347 HD/s)/(60 fps) = 0.0083339116 HD/f = 99.9833660884 HP; [HDNI] 0.5000347 HD/s *1.0000694/f = 0.50006940240818 HD/s

Frame 3 : [HP - HD] 99.9833660884 HP - (0.50006940240818 HD/s)/(60 fps) = 0.0083344900401363 HD/f = 99.9750315983598637 HP; [HDNI] 0.50006940240818 HD/s *1.0000694/f = 0.500104107224707127692 HD/s

However, this value assumes that the player isn't doing anything to benefit their health for those 8731 frames. We have another 2 options from the beginning of the save. The first of those is gathering berries, so let's discuss that.

Calculating estimated lifetime : Part 2 - Berries

In Increlution, you can gather 1 berry every 2 seconds from the beginning of a save file. This time will decrease as you gather more berries.

Eating a berry grants 5 HP, but can only be eaten once every 5 seconds. The 5-seconds rule applies to all food items in the game, but that isn't important currently.

Additionally, food will only be eaten when your HP is low enough to gain the full benefits of the food, or if eating it would prevent death (usually for a moment).

I don't currently understand how the last rule works as I haven't been able to trigger it in game. Perhaps it's intended for foods which heal more than your max HP?

Regardless, my idea to add this complexity is to assume that I have infinite berries (as I can only eat one every 5 seconds, but it takes no longer than 2 seconds to gather one.) & effectively add 5 HP every time HP is less than or equal to 95.
